Neutrino Quiz

Peter B. Denton

  1. Solar neutrinos at night:
    Are more neutrinos detected from the sun during the day or during the night?
    [answer]
    Night.

  2. Solar neutrino flavor ratios:
    For electron neutrinos from the sun, the flavor ratio at the Earth is close to 1:1:1. What is the expected flavor ratio for muon neutrinos produced in the sun?
    [answer]
    1:1:1. See this [twitter thread] for more.

  3. Distance to point of neutrino last scattering:
    The comoving distance to the photon surface of last scattering is O(10) Gpc. Is the surface of last scattering for neutrinos closer or farther?
    [answer]
    Closer. The temperature of last scattering is much higher for neutrinos than photons, but they don't travel far in the time it takes to cool down. So for massless neutrinos it's a tiny bit farther, but, for neutrinos heavier than about 0.0002 eV (and certainly some neutrinos are heavier than this), their finite mass moves this surface closer to us: O(1) Gpc away depending on their exact mass. Further details like oscillations make this effect a bit stronger. See [0907.2887]. Inspired by this [tweet].
