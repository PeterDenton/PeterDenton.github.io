Neutrino Quiz

Peter B. Denton

  1. Solar neutrinos at night:
    Are more neutrinos detected from the Sun during the day or during the night?
    [answer]
    Night.

  2. Solar neutrino flavor ratios:
    For electron neutrinos from the Sun, the flavor ratio at the Earth is close to 1:1:1. What is the expected flavor ratio for muon neutrinos produced in the Sun?
    [answer]
    1:1:1. See this [twitter thread] for more.

  3. Distance to point of neutrino last scattering:
    The comoving distance to the photon surface of last scattering is O(10) Gpc. Is the surface of last scattering for neutrinos closer or farther?
    [answer]
    Closer. The temperature of last scattering is much higher for neutrinos than photons, but they don't travel far in the time it takes to cool down. So for massless neutrinos it's a tiny bit farther, but, for neutrinos heavier than about 0.0002 eV (and certainly some neutrinos are heavier than this), their finite mass moves this surface closer to us: O(1) Gpc away depending on their exact mass. Further details like oscillations make this effect a bit stronger. See [0907.2887]. Inspired by this [tweet]. See this [twitter thread] for more.

  4. Possible solar measurements:
    SNO measured a CC to NC ratio of ~1/3 of solar neutrinos. KamLAND's measurement of reactor neutrinos confirmed the measurement of solar neutrinos by SNO and others. Given KamLAND's measurement, what other values if any could SNO have measured?
    [answer]
    2/3. Long-baseline reactor neutrino experiments like KamLAND and later JUNO measure sin22θ12 as the depth of the oscillation minima. High energy solar neutrinos E≳5 MeV are measured at a rate sin2θ12 which can take two different values given KamLAND's measurement. See this [twitter thread] for more.

  5. Massless neutrinos (Aug 2023):
    Given the current neutrino data, it could be that one of the mass states is massless. The massless state could be ν1. Which of the other two states, if any, could also be massless? (Define the mass states in the usual way: |Ue1|>|Ue2|>|Ue3|.)
    [answer]
    Just ν3. Put another way, we know that ν2 is massive since solar neutrino data from Homestake, SNO, SuperK, Borexino, SAGE, and GALEX tells us that Δm221 is positive. Since the atmospheric mass ordering (the sign of Δm231) is unknown, it could be that either ν1 or ν3 is massless. See this [twitter thread] for more (including plots).
